RESOURCE 09 · INTERVIEW

The Why This Role Framework

Three separate questions, each with its own research. Answer them one at a time and you stop sounding like every other candidate.

WHY THIS COMPANY
Research the company
What's actually happening here, not what the careers page says.
Where to look
  • Last 2 earnings calls or funding announcements
  • The CEO's recent posts and podcast interviews
  • Their engineering or product blog
  • Glassdoor and Blind, but only for the patterns
Lands when…
"You just shifted from X to Y, and that's the kind of bet I want to be part of building."
WHY THIS ROLE
Research the role
The actual work, the team, the surface area you'd own.
Where to look
  • The JD, read twice; the top 3 things they need
  • The hiring manager's LinkedIn and recent posts
  • The team's repos, talks, or shipped features
  • The recruiter screen, mined for hints on priorities
Lands when…
"Owning the migration off the legacy pipeline is exactly the kind of work I want my next step to be."
WHY NOW
Research yourself
Your timing and career arc. Why this, why now, why not next year.
Where to look
  • What you've outgrown in your current role
  • The skill or scope you're reaching for next
  • What changed recently (team, scope, market)
  • Why this opportunity beats staying put
Lands when…
"I've spent three years going deep on infra; I'm ready to apply it to a product surface with real users."
COMMON FUMBLES
"I'm just looking for a great opportunity."
"I want to grow."
"I'm passionate about [your domain]."
"I've heard good things about the team."
"It's a great company."
"I just want my next role."
